Aschaffenburg, of course, does not assert that Freud's importance ends with his theory of hysteria. But the medical public (psychiatrists included) know Freud mainlyfrom this side of his work, and for this reason adverse criticism could easily throw a shadow on Freud's other scientific achieve ments. I would like to remark at the start that my reply is notdirected to Aschaffenburg personally, but to the whole school of thought whose views and aspirations have found eloquentexpression in Aschaffenburg's lecture.

His criticism is confined exclusively to the role which sexuality, according to Freud, plays in the formation of the psychoneuroses.
